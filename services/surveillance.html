The Problem

Most Commercial Camera Systems Are Failing Silently

Low-resolution cameras on analog coax. DVRs nobody has checked in two years. Hard drives that failed months ago but nobody noticed. Blind spots at every entry point that matters.

When an incident happens — theft, liability claim, trespassing — your security team pulls footage and finds corrupted files, a camera pointing at a wall, or 24-hour overwrite when you needed 30-day retention. That system didn't fail the day of the incident. It was never designed correctly to begin with.

Our Approach

Every Camera Placed with Purpose. Every System Designed to Deliver.

We begin with a site survey and coverage map — identifying all entry and exit points, high-value areas, blind spot risks, and lighting conditions. Camera selection, placement, and field of view are determined by design before the first cable is pulled.

Every system deploys on dedicated network infrastructure with proper PoE budget, adequate storage retention, and remote access configured and verified before handoff. You know exactly what you have, where it covers, and how long footage is retained.

System Integration

Surveillance That Talks to the Rest of Your Infrastructure

We integrate surveillance with access control — a door forced open triggers a camera review automatically. We integrate with your network — surveillance VLANs properly segmented and bandwidth reserved. We build on the structured cabling backbone — every camera run certified and documented.

The result is a cohesive security system, not a camera system and a separate door system on a network nobody planned.

Access Control Integration

Forced-entry door events automatically pull camera footage from associated cameras — delivered to your security team as a correlated alert.

Network Architecture

Surveillance traffic segmented on dedicated VLANs with PoE budgeted correctly — no camera goes offline because of a switch overrun.

Structured Cabling

Every camera home-runs to a central IDF on certified Cat6A. Fiber backbone extends coverage to buildings and remote structures without signal loss.
